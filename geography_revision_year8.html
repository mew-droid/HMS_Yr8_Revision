Chapter 1: Development Indicators

Learn how we measure quality of life and compare countries

📚 What You Need to Know
Development Indicators are measurements that help us understand how developed a country is and what life is like for people living there.
👶
Birth Rate: Number of babies born per 1000 people per year. Higher in LEDCs (Less Economically Developed Countries).
💀
Death Rate: Number of deaths per 1000 people per year. Lower in MEDCs (More Economically Developed Countries).
🎂
Life Expectancy: Average age people live to. Higher in developed countries with better healthcare.
🍎
Calorie Intake: How much food people eat daily. Shows if people have enough nutrition.
👨‍⚕️
People per Doctor: How many people share one doctor. Lower number = better healthcare access.
📖
Adult Literacy: % of adults who can read and write. Higher in countries with good education.

Chapter 2: Tropical Rainforests

Understand ecosystem structure and how plants adapt

📚 Rainforest Layers
Tropical rainforests have distinct vertical layers, each with different plants, animals, and conditions. The tallest trees can reach 60 metres!
🌳
Emergent Layer (50-60m): Tallest trees poke through the canopy. Get most sunlight but exposed to wind and rain.
🌿
Canopy (30-50m): Dense layer of treetops. Most animals live here. Forms a "roof" blocking sunlight below.
🍃
Understory (10-30m): Smaller trees, shrubs. Dark and humid. Young trees waiting to grow tall.
🌱
Forest Floor (0-10m): Very dark. Only 2% of sunlight reaches here. Decomposition happens fast.

Chapter 3: Plate Tectonics

Earthquakes, volcanoes, and their impacts on people

📚 Understanding Plate Boundaries
The Earth's crust is broken into huge pieces called tectonic plates. Where plates meet, we get earthquakes and volcanoes. There are three types of plate boundaries.
💥
Destructive: Plates collide. Denser oceanic plate sinks under continental plate. Creates volcanoes and earthquakes.
↔️
Constructive: Plates move apart. Magma rises to fill gap. Creates new crust and underwater volcanoes.
🔀
Conservative: Plates slide past each other. Friction builds up then releases. Causes earthquakes but NO volcanoes.
🌊
Ocean Trench: Deep underwater valley where oceanic plate sinks. Found at destructive boundaries.

Chapter 5: Climate Zones

Reading and interpreting climate graphs

📚 Climate Graph Skills
Climate graphs show temperature (line) and rainfall (bars) for each month. Learn to recognize patterns quickly!
🌧️
Equatorial: Hot all year (25-28°C). High rainfall every month (200mm+). No dry season.
🏜️
Hot Desert: Hot in summer (40°C+), warm in winter. Very low rainfall all year (under 250mm total).
🌤️
Mediterranean: Hot dry summers, mild wet winters. Rainfall concentrated in winter months.
🌦️
British: Cool all year (max 18°C). Rain spread evenly across months. Small temperature range.

Chapter 6: Italy & Migration

Golden Triangle and internal migration patterns

📚 The Golden Triangle
The Golden Triangle is the industrial region in northwest Italy containing three major cities: Milan, Turin, and Genoa. This area has excellent transport links and attracts workers from southern Italy.
🏭
Milan: Italy's largest city. Centre of fashion and banking. Financial hub.
🚗
Turin: Industrial city. Fiat car manufacturing base. Engineering centre.
🚢
Genoa: Major port. Trade passes through. Gateway to Europe.
🏛️
Venice: Tourist city. Famous canals and old buildings. Outside the Golden Triangle.

Chapter 7: Earth & Seasons

Understanding Earth's orbit and tilt

📚 Key Concepts
Earth's tilt (23.5°) and its orbit around the Sun cause the seasons. Different parts of Earth receive different amounts of sunlight throughout the year.
☀️
Equator: Always gets DIRECT sun rays. Hot all year. No real seasons.
❄️
Poles: Sun rays hit at LOW angle. Spread over larger area. Very cold.
🌍
March Equinox: Sun overhead at equator. Equal day/night everywhere. Spring in Northern Hemisphere.
🌞
June Solstice: Sun overhead at Cancer (23.5°N). Summer in Northern Hemisphere. Longest day.
